# Math Thesis

Rule-Only Counterfactual Move-Delta Bottleneck (CDBN)

Source packet: `ideas/research_packets/chess_nn_research_2026-04-21_0436_tuesday_los_angeles_move_delta_bottleneck.md`.

## Working thesis

A position is puzzle-like when the side-to-move's rule-only one-ply counterfactual move-delta distribution is *sparse* and *anisotropic*: a few interventions look structurally different from the ordinary legal-looking background, even without engine scores or search. Non-puzzles are hypothesised to have a less anisotropic move-delta distribution after controlling for material and mobility.

## Formal object

For a board state `x` let `M(x)` be the set of pseudo-legal side-to-move interventions generated by chess movement rules without king-safety filtering or terminal-state tests. For each `m in M(x)` let `T_m x` be the post-move board and `delta_m(x) = T_m x - x` the sparse board delta. The model represents `delta_m` through a learned response vector `r_theta(x, m)` that combines the parent-board context vector `z_theta(x)` with the source/destination/path features of `delta_m`.

## Permutation-invariant sparse bottleneck

Let `s_theta(x, m)` be a learned per-move score and let `alpha_theta(x) = sparsemax({s_theta(x, m) / tau : m in M(x)})` be the masked sparsemax projection of the temperature-scaled scores onto the simplex. The move-cone bottleneck is

```
b_sparse(x) = sum_{m in M(x)} alpha_theta(x, m) * r_theta(x, m)
```

augmented with the masked mean `b_mean = mean_m r_m`, the masked second moment `b_second = mean_m r_m * r_m`, and the anisotropy scalar `kappa(x) = max_m s_theta(x, m) - logmeanexp_m s_theta(x, m)` (`logmeanexp` rather than `logsumexp` to suppress direct dependence on move count). The classifier consumes `[z, b_sparse, b_mean, b_second, kappa]`.

## Representation theorem

By Deep Sets the aggregator is a permutation-invariant continuous function of the move-delta multiset, and sparsemax-weighted sums can approximate extreme-element statistics (for separated scores the projection assigns positive mass only to a finite active set). Together these give the model class enough capacity to express "there exists a move delta in a learned tactical cone, and most other deltas are outside it" while staying invariant to generated move ordering.

## Hypotheses

- Puzzle-likeness in the current CRTK sample is correlated with sparse anisotropy of one-ply pseudo-legal move deltas.
- The sparse move-cone bottleneck suppresses material/source artifacts better than a static CNN.
- The central falsification ablation is the degree-preserving cross-position move-delta shuffle, which destroys the semantics of `T_m x - x` while preserving move counts and moving/captured-piece histograms.
