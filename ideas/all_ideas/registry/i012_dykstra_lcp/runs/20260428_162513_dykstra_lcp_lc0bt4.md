## What Was Implemented

This run implements the second recommended idea after VetoSelect: a compact Soft-Dykstra Latent Constraint Projector.

The model uses an LC0 BT4-style residual trunk, then predicts latent role, relation, motif, and slack variables. A four-cycle unrolled Dykstra-style projector applies box, simplex, role budget, role compatibility, relation geometry, endpoint compatibility, closure, and compactness projections. The classifier sees the trunk embedding plus before/after projection summaries and solver trace diagnostics.

The trainer uses `DykstraLCPLoss`, a binary BCE objective with hard-negative weighting plus residual regularization: positives are encouraged to project closer to the learned feasible set, while negatives receive a projection margin.

## What The Diagnostics Identify

The latent solver diagnostics are monotonic in the intended direction. True puzzles have lower projection distance and lower residuals than near-puzzles, while near-puzzles sit between known non-puzzles and verified puzzles.

| Fine Label | Meaning | Mean Prob Puzzle | Projection Distance | Trace Residual | Slack Mean | Role Mass | Relation Mass | Motif Entropy |
| ---: | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 0 | known non-puzzle | 0.1840 | 0.0463 | 0.0061 | 0.0428 | 7.2597 | 2.1313 | 1.0361 |
| 1 | verified near-puzzle | 0.3576 | 0.0313 | 0.0043 | 0.1005 | 7.5552 | 2.0841 | 1.4184 |
| 2 | verified puzzle | 0.7735 | 0.0188 | 0.0028 | 0.1857 | 7.8936 | 1.9433 | 1.5238 |

Interpretation: the projector learns a meaningful ordering of latent feasibility. Positions that look more puzzle-like project closer to the learned constraint set. The failure is not that the Dykstra trace is random; the failure is that near-puzzle hard negatives also project too close, so the classifier converts too much near-puzzle structure into positive probability.

It is weakest on the central benchmark pressure test: equal or hard positions where near-puzzle negatives look tactically sharp but are not verified puzzles. Promotions/underpromotions show the opposite failure: many true puzzles are missed.

## Decision

Status: tested, not promoted as the current best architecture.

Keep the implementation because it provides useful solver-trace diagnostics and a different mechanism for future hybrids. Do not replace VetoSelect v2 with this version. The next useful version would combine Dykstra diagnostics with an explicit near-puzzle veto/select head, or train an auxiliary near-puzzle ordering loss so class 1 is not treated merely as lower-confidence class 2.
