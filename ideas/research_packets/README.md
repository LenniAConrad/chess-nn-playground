## Prompt Lessons Applied

- Require a smallest central falsification ablation in the executive summary.
- Clarify safe rule-derived pseudo-legal geometry versus leakage-prone legal-move/search/oracle features.
- Require explicit encoding-adapter assumptions and fail-closed behavior.
- Require semantics-destroying or degree-preserving randomized ablations for structured operators.
- Require fine-label `0/1/2 -> predicted 0/1` diagnostics for main models and central ablations.
- Add near-puzzle diagnostics at matched fine-label-`0` false-positive rate where feasible.
- For rule-generated move-set models, require count-only and degree/material/side-preserving semantic-randomization ablations.
- For generated-move models, require memory and chunking estimates for `O(batch * max_moves * move_dim)` style workloads.
- For optimal-transport models, require product-coupling, randomized-cost, and marginal-only ablations.
- For nuisance-removal models, require projection-off and nuisance-only probes.
- For ordinal/evidential heads, require comparison against same-parameter unconstrained softmax or independent binary heads and class-`1` diagnostics.
- For sparse witness/rationale bottlenecks, require matched random/top-material witness ablations and ensure the classifier cannot see the full uncensored board.
- For ray-language models, require token-shuffle or geometry-destroying ray ablations.
- For higher-order constellation models, require degree-1-only and parameter-matched filler ablations.
- For pseudo-likelihood/MDL classifiers, require randomized-neighborhood and unary/material-only pseudo-likelihood controls.
- For symmetry/orbit quotient models, require exact channel-transform tests, semantics-destroyed pseudo-orbit controls, and augmentation-only controls.
- For side-to-move intervention models, require identity/random-side twins and explicit out-of-distribution critique for artificial counterfactual views.
- For credal/evidential ambiguity models, require hard-positive BCE or ordinary softmax controls and exported uncertainty/evidence diagnostics.
- For causal-invariance partition models, require random-environment controls and no-invariance/material-only/color-only controls.
- For kinematic operator-algebra models, require symmetric-product, first-order-only, and degree-preserving randomized-operator ablations.
- For masked-codec models, require unigram/material-prior and square-shuffled surprise controls.
- For cubical Euler/Betti topology models, require rank/histogram-preserving topology-destroying shuffles and count-only or individual-curve controls.
- For Hall/matching/overload models, require degree-matched edge rewires and obligation-count/material-only controls.
- For king-cage or king-escape path-DP models, require ring/bin-preserving hazard shuffles plus random-graph topology controls.
- For formal-context/FCA closure models, require row/column-sum-preserving incidence rewires and marginal-only controls.
- For denoising-score or generative-prior models, require all-class-prior, nuisance-only-prior, and random/permuted-field controls.
- For non-backtracking or edge-walk graph models, require degree/type-preserving randomized transition controls and count/degree/material-only controls.
